Magnolia Bakery's Famous Banana Pudding Recipe

Magnolia Bakery has become just as famous for its banana pudding as those cupcakes that appeared on "Sex and the City" a decade ago. Now Magnolia Bakery is sharing its recipe for the beloved dessert so that you can enjoy it at home - even if you're nowhere near one of the bakery's seven open US locations.

To make Magnolia Bakery's classic banana pudding, you'll need:

  • 4 to 5 bananas, sliced
  • 11-ounce box of vanilla wafers (Nilla Wafers are recommended)
  • 3 cups of heavy cream
  • 1 package of instant vanilla-pudding mix (Jell-O is recommended)
  • 14 ounce can of sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 1/2 cups of ice-cold water

Directions:

  1. To begin Magnolia's recipe, start by whisking the milk and cold water.
  2. Add the pudding mix into a separate bowl and slowly whisk in your milk-and-water mixture for about a minute, until it's smooth and lumpless.
  3. Cover the bowl and refrigerate it for at least an hour. You can also just leave it overnight.
  4. Once your pudding mixture is ready, whisk your heavy cream until stiff peaks form.
  5. Add the pudding to your whipped cream and mix until they've fully blended.
  6. Spread the quarter of your pudding over the bottom of a bowl.(Bobbie Lloyd, Magnolia's chief baking officer, recommends using a trifle bowl or a wide glass bowl.) Add a layer of vanilla wafers and enough sliced bananas on top to cover them. Repeat twice.
  7. To finish, add one final layer of pudding and any of your additional cookies or crumbs on top.
  8. Then cover your bowl with a plastic wrap and refrgerate it for four to six hours.
